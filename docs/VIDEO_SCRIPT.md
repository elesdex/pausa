# Demo video plan — target 2:35

The final video must remain under three minutes and include voiceover, a working product demo, how Codex was used, and what GPT-5.6 does.

## 0:00–0:18 — the human problem

**Visual:** Show `public/demo/bank-alert-es.png`, the clearly labeled synthetic bank message: “A deposit is being processed. Call now if you do not recognize it.”

**Voiceover:**

> Scam messages use urgency to make us act before we think. This is especially frightening when someone we love does not feel confident with technology. HELP is a free, open-source help button for that moment.

## 0:18–0:38 — pause before analysis

**Visual:** Open HELP from the phone home screen. Show the Spanish landing screen, the large pause symbol, and “Revisar algo sospechoso.”

**Voiceover:**

> HELP does not monitor private messages. The person opens it only when they want help. The first instruction is simple: do not respond yet.

## 0:38–1:08 — accessible input

**Visual:** Show the four paths: camera, screenshot, pasted text, and voice. Use the guided example or upload a synthetic screenshot.

**Voiceover:**

> You do not need to know how to take a screenshot. You can photograph another screen, choose an image, paste the message, or describe it by voice.

## 1:08–1:38 — GPT-5.6 result

**Visual:** Submit the message. Show the calm loading state, high-risk result, warning signals, safe steps, and read-aloud button.

**Voiceover:**

> GPT-5.6 reads the text and image, identifies possible warning signals, and returns a constrained result. It never claims certainty. Here it notices urgency, a phone number supplied by the message, and a financial fear trigger. Instead of calling that number, HELP guides us to leave the message and verify through a channel we already trusted.

## 1:38–1:53 — bilingual and installable

**Visual:** Switch to English. Briefly show “Add to Home Screen” or the installed standalone app.

**Voiceover:**

> HELP works in Spanish and English, reads the guidance aloud, and installs as a mobile web app without an app store.

## 1:53–2:22 — Codex story

**Visual:** Fast montage of the primary Codex task, objective, roadmap, code changes, test output, and build log. Keep all private data hidden.

**Voiceover:**

> I am not a traditional software developer. I began by talking through the idea while walking around my house. Codex turned that conversation into a product objective, checked the rules, designed and built the app, implemented the GPT-5.6 integration, caught a misleading demo fallback, created safety evaluations, ran the build and tests, and maintained the evidence for this submission.

## 2:22–2:35 — mission and close

**Visual:** HELP logo, public URL, GitHub repository, Apache 2.0 mark.

**Voiceover:**

> Scams use urgency. HELP gives people calm and one safer next step. It is free, open source, and ready for families and communities to improve together.

## Final recording checklist

- Use only synthetic or redacted messages.
- Show a real live-analysis result, not only the deterministic demo.
- Keep browser and phone notifications hidden.
- Show the English interface for at least one clear shot.
- Show Codex working, not only finished files.
- Include the public app and repository URLs on the closing frame.
- Export at 1080p and verify the final duration is under 3:00.
